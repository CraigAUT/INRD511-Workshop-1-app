Level 2 · GET STARTED

Make a real start on the assignment

In POOL 1 (Week 3) you present early work on three things: user personas and research, desk research into the context, and moodboards of possible 2027 trends. This level unpacks the brief and starts you on all three.

Where are you with the brief right now?

Honest answer.

The brief in your own words

Two sentences: what you're making, and what it has to do. Have the brief open next to this. If your version turns out wrong, that's a POOL 1 question.

Stuck? See the shape of it

The shape: I'm making [what] for [who]. It has to [the requirement that matters most] and I'll show my thinking through [the deliverable].

"I'm making a small interactive experience that helps first-years find quiet study spaces on campus. It has to work on a phone, and I'll show my thinking through my process journal."

That's an invented project. Yours comes from the real brief.

One thing that's unclear

Something in the brief you'd want confirmed or explained. If nothing, write what you'd double-check.

Stuck? See an example
"The brief mentions the experience working across the event. Does that mean before and after the day itself, or only on site?"

Unclear things make good POOL 1 questions.

Who could this be for?

List two or three user groups you can imagine. Rough labels are fine.

Stuck? See an example
"First-time attendees still studying; speakers preparing sessions; a studio sending a small team."

Your first research move

These map onto POOL 1. Pick one to start today; you'll do all of them before Week 3.

Name your source and note why it earns a place

One or two sentences in your own words.

Stuck? See an example note
"Norman's chapter on affordances gives me language for why an interface can confuse people even when it technically works. Useful for explaining my design decisions, not just showing them."

Try the reference optional here, required in your document

Write the APA reference as best you can. Rough is fine. Build it with the APA Reference Builder, or check it later with the APA Referencing Agent in Canvas.

What a finished one looks like
Norman, D. (2013). The design of everyday things (Rev. ed.). Basic Books.

Author, year, title in italics, publisher. The agent will pick up anything that's off.

Pick one group: their situation

Two sentences on what's going on for them. This is the seed of a persona.

Stuck? See an example
"This is for first-year students new to the city. Most are juggling study with part-time work and don't yet know where anything is, so anything that adds effort gets abandoned fast."

The second sentence describes their situation. That's what context writing does.

Your next step before Week 3

One line: the next thing you want to find out, and where you'll look. The starter questions cover all three strands.

Starter questions for each strand

Users (personas): Who attends an event like this? What do they want from it? What gets in their way?

Context (desk research): What comparable events exist? Who's speaking, and on what? What's happening in design in Aotearoa right now?

Trends (moodboard): Which design systems and styles are gaining ground? What reads as 2027 rather than 2024?

To finish: make both choices and fill every box. The reference attempt is optional.

Level 3 · ASK

Write the question you'll bring to POOL 1

In POOL 1 you present to a small group, and you take one question with you. The question aims the feedback you get.

What do you most want a response on in POOL 1?

Pick one; the others can wait.

Who's best placed to answer it?

Pick one for this question.

Now write it, one sentence

A shape that works: Can you look at [the specific thing] and tell me [what you want to know about it]?

Weak and strong versions

Weak:

"Is this good?"

Gets you "yeah, it's fine".

Strong:

"Can you look at my two personas and tell me which one feels least like a real person, and why?"

Names the thing, says what response would help, answerable in two minutes.

Check it against three tests

If one doesn't hold, edit the question until it does.

To finish: make both choices, write the question, and tick all three tests.
